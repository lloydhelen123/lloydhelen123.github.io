Activate Space

Bringing spaces and people together

Project Date
2016
Location
London, UK
Organisers
Land Insight, with Future Cities Catapult
Role
Participant; Designer
Team Members
Daisy Falconer, Ben Eley (City of London), Adam Roberts (Karakusevic Carson), Esther Cheng (Amazon)

As part of a 'Planning Hackathon' organised by Land Insight and the Future Cities Catapult, our team proposed a data-driven tool that would identify and activate underutilised real estate in London.

Vacant lots and underutilised property is a public eyesore and contributes to a negative urban streetscape. They do not generate income for the landowner and are often a liablity in terms of maintenance and upkeep. Although numerous organisations are founded on a supply of this type of property, their methods of identifying potential sites is archaic. We proposed to arm these groups with data-driven tools, enabling quick and easily identification of opportunities, as and when they need them, acting as a catalyst in the chain. Ultimately, we proposed to Activate Space.

The Business

Provide a service as a percentage commission

Our business model is simple: Using our proprietry multi-layered data analysis methodology, we identify suitable real estate, before funnelling this down an existing, established supply chain, taking a percentage commission. Using our expertise we can greater enable these organisations and businesses.




The Data

Our multi-Layered search methodologies utilise existing data

There is no straightforward method for identifying suitable property. Traditionally, this has been achieved by land scouts physically scouring the city and following up on leads. Our product combines data from local planning applications, the Valuation Office Agency and more rudimentary forces, such as utilising Freedom of Information Act, to positively identify suitable opportunities for the supply chain.
