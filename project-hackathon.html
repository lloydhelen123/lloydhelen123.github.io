Activate Space

Bringing spaces and people together

Project Date
2016
Location

Organisers

Role
Participant; Designer
Team Members
Daisy Falconer, Ben Eley (City of London), Adam Roberts (Karakusevic Carson), Esther Cheng (Amazon)

As part of a 'Planning Hackathon' organised by Land Inisght, a company that uses data to clarify land ownership details, and the Future Citeis Catapult, a government-supported centre for the advancement of smart cities, our team proposed a data-driven tool to identify and activate underutilised real estate in the city.

It is common in to see vacant lots throughout. While this can be for a myriad reasons, we proposed that intelligent use of open-source data can unlock these spaces for temporary and adaptive re-use. Although numberous (typically non-profit) organisations exist purely feed off of these vancant and underutilised sites, their method for identify them is archaic. We propoese to arm them with a data-driven tool that would make locating opportunities simpler, faster, and more efficient.

The Solution

Drive the efficiency and potential of an existing market

A myriad of organisations including Appear Here, Camelot, and the Camden Collective live off vacant and underutilised land. Increasingly, commercial operation such as Deliveroo are seeking temporary off-site space as atypical business models emerge within the new economy. Our goal was to connect these groups to suitable vacant and underutilised real estate.

Business Model

Provide a service as a percentage commission

Our business model is simple: Using our proprietry multi-layered data analysis methodology, we identify suitable real estate. We then funnel this down to an established supply chain, taking a percentage commission. Using our expertise we can greater enable these organisations and businesses.
